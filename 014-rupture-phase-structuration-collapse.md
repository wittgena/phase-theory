## 1. Overview

This document reframes **rupture** not as the endpoint of collapse,  
but as the **phase-saturated field where collapse and Dominium co‑emerge**.

Collapse becomes visible as rupture only when:

- phase drift saturates the interference field (Φ),  
- recursive judgment (Theoria) fails or attenuates,  
- drifted Ψ‑loops converge into a chaotic attractor (Φ‑chaotic → Φ‑breach),  
- and simultaneously, Theoria attempts re-entry.

> **Rupture is the moment where disalignment gains form,  
> and Dominium is the moment where reflection returns within that form.**  
These two are *not sequential*, but **phase-synchronized events** within the same rupture surface.

This structure aligns with:

- **PIR** — interference saturation leading to recursive destabilization  
- **RPCP** — collapse through judgment loss  
- **PSH** — gluing failure and chaotic attractors  
- **Isorhesis** — drift → reflection → recursive re-coherence

$$
