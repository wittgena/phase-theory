# Orthogonality in Phase-Theory: Topological Interference and Recursive Coherence

## 1. Overview

In the Phase-Theory framework, **orthogonality** describes a special condition in which two distinct phase flows — the internal loop of recursive judgment and the external loop of environmental feedback — **intersect topologically without collapsing into each other**. This crossing is not destructive interference, but rather the origin of **recursive tension**, which leads to **coherent topological structures** such as the torus.

Rather than a metaphor, orthogonality in this context becomes a **phase-structural attractor**, forming the critical interface between drift, judgment, and ontological anchoring.

```text
L → M → P → T → (L)
```

This external flow represents **environmental, systemic, or mythic pressures** entering the judgment loop.
